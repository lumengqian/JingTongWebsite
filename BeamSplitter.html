Introduction of Beam Splitter

A beamsplitter is an optical instrument that can split a beam of light into two by reflecting or transmitting the light. It is an important component of many optical instruments (such as interferometers) and fiber-optic telecommunications equipment.

Working Mechanism of Beam Splitter

Common beam splitters are made of two glass triangular prisms bonded diagonally into a glass cube. The adhesive is usually made of polyester, epoxy or polyurethane. The thickness of the adhesive is precisely adjusted to form total internal reflection, so that when a beam of a specific wavelength enters from one side of the beam splitter, half of it will be transmitted and the other half will be reflected.

Another design is to coat an optical thin film on a glass or plastic substrate. The thin film is designed so that when light of a specific wavelength is directed toward the substrate at a 45-degree angle, half is transmitted and half is reflected, thereby equally dividing the incident light beam.

The third type is the dichroic prism. By combining different prisms, a dichroic coating is used to separate the incident light into several beams of light with different wavelengths. It is usually used in camera tubes, special art effect and other applications.
